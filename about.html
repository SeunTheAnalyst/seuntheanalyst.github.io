About Me

I graduated from Babcock University, Nigeria, with a Bachelor’s degree in Mass Communication in 2017. At Babcock, I took courses like Data Analysis in Communication Research, Statistics for Social Science, Introduction to Information Science, Introduction to Advertising and Public Relations, Communication Research, and Communication and Society—each of which, in their own specific ways, provided me with a detailed exposition on how media works and how its many activities affect our society, shape both individual and collective consciousness, and our overall perception of the world. These preliminary studies offered me an in-depth understanding of the foundational principles and ethics that guide the broad church of Mass Communication, so that when I applied for a Master’s degree in Communications and International Marketing at the University of Surrey, United Kingdom, I excelled at it.

I obtained my Master’s degree in 2018, a much improved, skilled version of myself. What drives me, to put it simply, is growth. I am always seeking ways to get better, to stand out and be named amongst the best of the best at what I do in the world, hence my predisposition to always seize every opportunity I can, whether in academia or through on-the-job experiences, to acquire knowledge.
